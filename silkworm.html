Silkworms: Life Cycle and Silk Production

Silkworms are the larvae of the domesticated silkmoth known for their silk production.

Life Cycle of Silkworms

The life cycle of a silkworm consists of four main stages:

  1. Egg: The silkworm life cycle begins as eggs laid by the adult female silkmoth.
  2. Larva (Caterpillar): The hatched larva, commonly referred to as a silkworm, feeds on mulberry leaves voraciously, growing rapidly and molting several times.
  3. Pupa: The silkworm constructs a cocoon around itself using silk threads produced from its salivary glands. Inside the cocoon, it transforms into a pupa, undergoing metamorphosis.
  4. Adult Moth: The fully developed moth emerges from the cocoon. It is not capable of feeding and its sole purpose is reproduction.
